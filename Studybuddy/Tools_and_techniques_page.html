Information about tools and techniques which are
for study are written below

Tools

NZQA website

A website which shows your total credits for the year. It presents your progress in both graphs which allows you to see at a glance the spread of your credits as it shows how many you have for each grade and a more detailed table which shows the grade and number of credits for the subjects you take giving the user an in depth look at your progress, helping you manage your credit numbers. Students can use this tool to check their internal and external assessment grades. It is recommended that all students check this website regularly to keep track of their current and pending credits, Currently, students need 80 credits to pass level 1 and 60 credits for levels 2 and 3. All year levels need 50 Merit or Excellence credits to pass with Merit or Excellence Endorsements respectively. For subject endorsements, all students need 14 Merit or Excellence credits with at least 3 from Internals and 3 from Externals to get merit or Excellence subject endorsement respectively.

Remember the milk

A detailed to-do list website which will aid you in adding tasks to be completed with the feature to categorize and assign due dates to them. This will be very useful for students who are struggling to juggle extracurricular activities. The site will allow you to create separate lists for school and extracurriculars, aiding you to stay on top of all stresses of the school environment.

My Studylife

A website that keeps track of the subjects you take, your exams and tasks, organising them into a calendar. The website also tracks future exams and tasks due and puts them into the calendar helping with organisation as then, you can utilise it as a to-do list. The website also allows you to add any scheduled holidays helping with organisation as you would have time to plan ahead around the holiday and find any assessments that you will miss.

Techniques

Pomodoro

This is a technique involving breaking your study period into periods of around 25 minutes in length with a 10-minute break after each period. This technique is beneficial for those who easily get distracted and struggle to start a project by setting a 25 minute time period.

Past papers

Requires you to use past school papers for practice, which works well for NCEA students as the NZQA website provides many practice papers, dating back to 2014, meaning you have a lot of resources to do this.

Mind mapping

You create a diagram that lists all important topics and important notes in a large page and link them together. This works well for visual learners as they see the important information physically mapped out therefore can form a picture of the topic in their head to refer to in the future.

Spaced repetition

It is a method used for retaining information, where flashcards are organized into a box in 5 separate sections. To use this technique, you will need to answer the questions on the flashcards and when you get the question wrong, you will move that card to a more often visited section and when the question is right, it is moved down to a less-visited section. The end goal is to put all cards to the least visited section where the questions are answered correctly each time indicating the user has successfully retained the information.
